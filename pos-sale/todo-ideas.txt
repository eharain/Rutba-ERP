Report ideas for POS sale app
================================

1) Sales & Revenue Reports
- Daily/Weekly/Monthly sales summary (total sales, discounts, net revenue, invoices, avg ticket)
- Sales by product (qty sold, revenue, margin if cost available)
- Sales by category/brand
- Sales by payment method (Cash / Card / Bank / Mobile Wallet / Store Credit)
- Sales by branch/desk
- Top discounted items (total discount amount, avg discount per item, % of sales at discount)
- Discount usage by cashier/branch (who gives most discounts, average %)

2) Returns & Exchanges Reports
- Sale returns summary (count, total refund, return rate vs sales, avg refund)
- Returns by product (most returned items)
- Returns by status (Returned, ReturnedDamaged, Damaged, InStock)
- Returns by user/cashier processing the return
- Return vs original sale (sale total, refund total, days between sale and return)
- Damaged items report (items marked Damaged / ReturnedDamaged, original vs negotiated refund price)

3) Cash & Cash Register Reports
- End-of-day / Z report by cash register
  - Opening float, total sales, total refunds, payouts, expected closing balance, variance
- Cash movement report from cash-register-transactions
  - All In/Out/Refund movements with user and description
- Refunds by payment method (cash refunds vs non-cash)

4) Customer & Loyalty Reports
- Customer sales history (per customer: total spend, number of visits, last visit date, avg basket)
- Top customers by revenue for a period
- Customers with high return rates (potential abuse / quality issues)
-- Customer behaviour & segmentation ideas --
- Top buyers with low return rates (very valuable)
- High spenders with high return rates (possible product/fit issues)
- Customers who frequently buy but with small baskets (opportunity to upsell)
- Time-based behaviour: customers who mostly shop weekends vs weekdays
- Channel/desk preferences: which desks/branches each customer prefers
- Cohort-style view: first purchase month vs current activity
- RFM-style tags (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) to classify customers

5) Inventory & Stock Reports
- Stock on hand per product (qty, value at cost, value at selling price)
- Stock movement report (Purchase, Sale, Return, Adjustment, Damage)
- Slow/Non-moving items (no or low sales in period)
- Shrinkage / damage report (value lost to damaged items)

6) Performance & Operations Reports
- Cashier performance (sales count, revenue, refunds processed, avg basket, refund ratio)
- Desk/branch performance comparison
- Peak hours and days (based on invoice timestamps)

7) Audit & Compliance Reports
- Voided/cancelled sales (if tracked)
- Manual price overrides/discounts (if tracked)
- Returns without original receipt or outside policy window (if policy data available)

8) Technical/Implementation Notes
- Most reports should support filters: date range, branch, desk, cashier, payment method.
- Many can be implemented first as simple server-side JSON endpoints, then visualized in the sale app UI.
- For heavy queries, consider pre-aggregations or scheduled daily summary tables.
